# Planning & Logistics Reference

Detailed checklists and operational guides for hackathon organizers.

## Master Checklist

### Venue Requirements

- [ ] Reliable WiFi (test for 2x expected attendees concurrently)
- [ ] Enough power outlets or extension cords (1 per 2 seats minimum)
- [ ] Presentation area with projector/screen and sound system
- [ ] Breakout rooms for workshops and mentoring
- [ ] Accessible entrance and facilities (ADA/accessibility compliance)
- [ ] Quiet zone for overnight events (sleeping area)
- [ ] Secure area for sponsor equipment and valuables
- [ ] Loading dock or easy access for catering delivery
- [ ] HVAC that works after-hours (buildings often shut down climate control on weekends)
- [ ] 24/7 access if overnight (security badge or on-site contact)

### Registration Setup

- [ ] Online registration form collecting: name, email, school/company, experience level, dietary restrictions, t-shirt size, emergency contact
- [ ] Waiver/liability form (check with venue and legal)
- [ ] Check-in system for day-of (QR code, printed list, or app)
- [ ] Name badges/lanyards pre-printed or on-demand
- [ ] Swag bags ready for distribution at check-in

### Communication Plan

| When | What | Channel |
|------|------|---------|
| 2 weeks before | Event details, schedule, what to bring | Email |
| 1 week before | Final reminder, parking/transit info | Email |
| 1 day before | Excitement builder, last-minute info | Email + Discord |
| Day-of, hourly | Meals, workshops, deadline reminders | Discord + PA system |
| Post-event | Thank you, feedback survey, photos | Email |

### Volunteer Roles

| Role | Count | Responsibilities |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Registration desk | 2-3 | Check-in, badge distribution, wayfinding |
| Tech support | 2-3 | WiFi issues, AV setup, power strip distribution |
| Mentor coordinator | 1 | Route teams to available mentors, manage schedule |
| Food coordinator | 1-2 | Receive deliveries, set up meals, manage waste |
| Photography/video | 1-2 | Document the event for social media and sponsors |
| Floaters | 2-4 | Fill gaps, handle unexpected issues |

**Volunteer shifts:** 4-6 hour shifts max. Burned-out volunteers make mistakes.

## Food Planning

### Per-Person Estimates

| Meal | Budget | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Breakfast | $5-8 | Bagels, fruit, yogurt, coffee (lots of coffee) |
| Lunch | $8-12 | Pizza, sandwiches, or boxed lunches |
| Dinner | $10-15 | Catered meals (ethnic food variety works well) |
| Snacks | $3-5 | Energy bars, chips, fruit, candy |
| Drinks | $2-3 | Water, juice, soda, energy drinks |
| Coffee | $2-3 | Budget for continuous supply — this is critical |

### Dietary Accommodation

Always provide clearly labeled options for:
- Vegetarian (usually 15-25% of attendees)
- Vegan (5-10%)
- Halal
- Kosher
- Gluten-free
- Nut-free

**Pro tip:** Order 10-15% extra food. Running out of food is worse than having leftovers. Donate leftovers to a local shelter.

### Pizza Math

If ordering pizza: 2-3 slices per person. A large pizza has ~8 slices. For 100 people: 30-40 pizzas. Always include cheese, pepperoni, and at least 2 vegetarian options.

## Emergency Planning

- **First aid kit** on-site with bandages, pain relievers, antacids
- **Emergency contacts** posted visibly — local hospital, campus security, event lead phone numbers
- **Code of conduct** published and enforced — have a clear reporting process and at least 2 designated contacts
- **Fire exits** identified and communicated
- **Insurance** — check if venue insurance covers your event, or get event insurance ($200-500 for most hackathons)
